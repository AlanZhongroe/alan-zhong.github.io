  <!-- RESEARCH -->

  <section id="research">

    <div class="section-kicker">Research</div>

    <h2>Research directions</h2>

    <div class="two-col">

      <div>

        <p><strong>First stars and galactic building blocks</strong></p>

        <p>

          My PhD work will focus on the physical processes governing the

          first generations of stars and galaxies and how their feedback

          enriches and reshapes the surrounding medium. I am particularly

          interested in:

        </p>

        <ul>

          <li>How metals from Population III supernovae mix into nearby haloes</li>

          <li>When, where, and how metal-enriched (Pop II) stars can form</li>

          <li>The role of small “building block” systems in assembling larger galaxies</li>

        </ul>



        <p><strong>Auxiliary functionals &amp; kinetic theory</strong></p>

        <p>

          In parallel, I have been exploring rigorous bounding methods

          (auxiliary/背景流 functional approaches) for kinetic equations

          such as the Vlasov–Maxwell system, with applications to

          plasma devices and space plasmas. This work naturally connects

          mathematical analysis, numerical optimisation, and physical

          interpretation.

        </p>

      </div>

      <div>

        <p><strong>Methods &amp; tools</strong></p>

        <ul>

          <li>Hydrodynamical and N-body simulations</li>

          <li>Analysis of high-resolution cosmological simulation data</li>

          <li>Python, C/Fortran environments, and HPC workflows</li>

          <li>Lyapunov / background flow methods and sum-of-squares tools</li>

        </ul>



        <p><strong>Looking ahead</strong></p>

        <p>

          During my PhD I hope to combine detailed numerical simulations

          with semi-analytic models so that we can better connect early-time

          physics to upcoming observational constraints from facilities

          such as JWST and future 21-cm experiments.

        </p>

      </div>

    </div>

  </section>



  <!-- PUBLICATIONS / PROJECTS -->

  <section id="publications">

    <div class="section-kicker">Work</div>

    <h2>Selected projects &amp; writing</h2>



    <div class="pub-item">

      <div class="pub-title">Background flow / auxiliary functional methods for Vlasov equations</div>

      <div class="pub-meta">

        MSc Research Project · Imperial College London · 2024–2025

      </div>

      <p>

        Developed and tested auxiliary functional (background flow) methods

        to obtain rigorous bounds for kinetic systems such as the

        Vlasov–Maxwell equations, with an eye towards applications to Hall

        thrusters and space plasmas. This involved constructing Lyapunov-type

        functionals, computing their time derivatives, and exploring

        sum-of-squares formulations.

      </p>

    </div>



    <div class="pub-item">

      <div class="pub-title">Early-Universe metal enrichment and Pop II star formation</div>

      <div class="pub-meta">

        Ongoing reading &amp; pre-PhD project · University of Edinburgh

      </div>

      <p>

        Building a literature-based and simulation-informed overview of how

        metals from the first stars propagate, mix, and cool in primordial

        haloes, and what this implies for the first generations of

        metal-enriched stars and small galaxies.

      </p>

    </div>



    <div class="pub-item">

        interferometric techniques, gaining experience with data analysis,

        simulation tools, and the challenges of extracting cosmological

        signals.
